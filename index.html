Toward Interactive Texture Synthesis: A Multi-Scale Latent Trajectory Model

Abstract

Audio textures exhibit complex temporal dynamics that are not well represented by single-scale models. We propose a multi-scale latent trajectory approach for audio texture synthesis using representations from a pre-trained neural audio codec. By decomposing latent trajectories into slow structural and fast stochastic components, we model long-term dynamics with a variational autoencoder and short-term detail with a conditional autoregressive model.

Experiments on environmental audio textures show that the proposed method significantly improves audio quality over a single-scale baseline and achieves performance competitive with a latent diffusion model. Using perceptually motivated summary statistics, we demonstrate that generated continuations preserve texture identity.

The proposed method exposes a smooth latent space that enables deterministic control over long-term structure, supporting coherent interpolation and continuous generation from a given context.
